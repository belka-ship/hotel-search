# Hotel Descriptions — FiDi, New York

## 1. Gild Hall, A Thompson Hotel

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Tucked away on historic Gold Street, Gild Hall offers an intimate, "Aspen-meets-Manhattan" aesthetic originally conceived by designer Jim Walrod. The boutique property sets a sophisticated, club-like tone with masculine, library-inspired interiors featuring custom leather-tufted headboards, rich mahogany, brass accents, and tartan plaid throws. The lobby acts as a cozy retreat from the Financial District, featuring a striking chandelier and a warm, residential atmosphere.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** The 130 contemporary guest rooms are characterized by high ceilings and warm, moody color palettes. Rooms are outfitted with 400-thread-count SFERRA linens, fully stocked oversized minibars, flat-screen TVs, and Geneva sound systems. The marble-floored bathrooms feature premium toiletries and rainfall showers. Higher-tier suites elevate the experience with expansive living spaces and luxurious clawfoot soaking tubs.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The hotel is anchored by Felice Ristorante, an all-day dining venue serving rustic Tuscan fare, including upstate burrata, homemade meatballs, and an extensive Italian wine list. For evening relaxation, the upper-level La Soffitta wine bar offers soulful Italian dishes in a plush, lounge-like setting. Guests also have access to a 24-hour fitness center.

**User Review Highlights:** Guests consistently rave about the tranquil, intimate atmosphere that feels like an exclusive private club rather than a bustling corporate hotel. Reviewers frequently highlight the exceptionally comfortable beds, the high quality of the on-site Italian dining, and the warmly attentive, personalized service from the staff.

## 2. Hyatt Centric Wall Street

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Occupying a prominent landmark tower at the corner of Wall and Water streets, this property (formerly an Andaz) was originally designed by the Rockwell Group. It leans heavily into a modern, loft-style aesthetic. The hotel emphasizes a communal "living room" lobby concept where a host checks guests in via tablet, fostering a highly personalized, barrier-free welcome experience.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** This hotel boasts some of the largest entry-level rooms in New York City, starting at a generous size and expanding up to 1,465 square feet for premium suites. Rooms feature 11-foot ceilings, oversized bleached-wood window seats overlooking Lower Manhattan, and oak flooring. Tech amenities include automated blackout shades and Geneva sound systems. The ultra-spacious bathrooms are a major draw, featuring deep soaking tubs, separate rainfall showers, and exclusive Beekman 1802 bath products.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The Centric Bar & Restaurant focuses on farm-to-table American bistro cuisine and craft cocktails. During warmer months, the hotel opens a courtyard beer garden and hosts a local farmers' market. The property also features a fully equipped 24-hour fitness center and offers in-room spa services.

**User Review Highlights:** Reviews overwhelmingly focus on the unexpectedly massive room sizes and the spa-like, multi-zone bathrooms. Guests also highly rate the pet-friendly policies (which include dog beds and bowls), the spectacular views from the high-floor window seats, and the high-tech bedside controls.

## 3. Moxy NYC Downtown

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Located near the Oculus and Fulton Center, this Marriott-brand lifestyle hotel is entirely focused on high-energy, playful design. The architecture and interiors are distinctly youthful, featuring neon signage, raw concrete finishes, and bold art installations. The centerpiece is the 5,000-square-foot third-floor space known as "Recreation," which acts as a hybrid co-working space, lounge, and arcade.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** The rooms are micro-sized but highly functional, utilizing clever, space-saving design inspired by ship cabins. Furniture, such as desks and luggage racks, can be folded and hung on a pegboard wall to maximize floor space. Despite the compact footprint, rooms feature premium comfort items like 300-thread-count bedding, floor-to-ceiling windows, suspended hanging chairs in select rooms, automated lighting, and large walk-in showers with Muk toiletries.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The Recreation level features retro arcade games, skee-ball, and even a half-court indoor basketball court. The vibrant bar serves creative cocktails and hosts live DJs, making it a major nightlife hub. A 24-hour modern fitness center equipped with Peloton bikes and punching bags is also available.

**User Review Highlights:** Younger travelers and couples highly praise the energetic, lively atmosphere and the engaging communal spaces. Reviewers love the innovative room design, the panoramic city views from the higher floors, and the undeniable value for the location.

## 4. Courtyard by Marriott Downtown

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Situated right next to the World Trade Center site and the 9/11 Memorial, this modern, 317-room high-rise is built for absolute convenience and reliability. The property features a clean, contemporary design typical of the updated Courtyard brand, prioritizing functional public spaces and straightforward navigation for both business and leisure travelers.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** Rooms are bright, soundproofed, and feature large windows—many offering direct views of the One World Trade Center or the city skyline. Amenities include ergonomic workstations, sitting areas, flat-screen TVs with premium channels, and coffee-making facilities. The decor is understated and comfortable, with carpeted floors and modern, well-lit bathrooms featuring complimentary toiletries.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The hotel features the brand's signature Bistro, offering hearty breakfast options, specialty Starbucks coffee, and casual dinner and cocktail service in the evening. Guests have access to a well-maintained gym equipped with cardio machines and free weights. A 24-hour convenience market is located in the lobby for grab-and-go snacks.

**User Review Highlights:** The most frequently mentioned highlight is the unbeatable transit connectivity, as the hotel sits directly in front of the WTC subway entrances. Guests also consistently praise the high standards of room cleanliness, the reliable Wi-Fi, and the friendly, accommodating front desk staff.

## 5. Holiday Inn NYC Wall Street

**Property Overview & Architecture:** This 15-story property offers a straightforward, no-frills approach to hospitality in the heart of the Financial District. Located slightly off the main thoroughfares, it provides a quieter street environment while remaining just blocks from the New York Stock Exchange. The lobby is modest and functional, designed to get guests checked in and on their way efficiently.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** The 113 guest rooms are traditionally furnished, prioritizing cleanliness and basic comforts. Each room includes a work desk, flat-screen TV, ironing facilities, and an in-room safe. While on the smaller side, the rooms are well-maintained and feature comfortable bedding, blackout curtains, and standard en-suite bathrooms.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The hotel features the Hobnoben restaurant, which serves standard American fare for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. A major perk for families is the brand's "Kids Eat Free" policy. The property also boasts a rooftop bar that, while modest, offers excellent skyline views of Lower Manhattan. A 24-hour business center is available for corporate travelers.

**User Review Highlights:** Reviews highlight this hotel as a highly reliable, budget-friendly option in an otherwise expensive zip code. Guests frequently compliment the surprisingly good views from the rooftop, the quietness of the rooms, and the overall cleanliness and comfort provided for the price point.

## 6. DoubleTree by Hilton Downtown

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Located near Battery Park and the historic dining enclave of Stone Street, this high-rise Hilton property is a classic full-service hotel. It caters heavily to corporate travelers and cruise passengers (due to its proximity to the ferry terminals). The decor is traditional and professional, with a sprawling lobby designed to handle high volumes of guests.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** Rooms are generally spacious by New York standards and feature warm, traditional decor with mahogany-toned furnishings. Amenities include the signature Sweet Dreams by DoubleTree plush bedding, large work desks with ergonomic chairs, coffee makers, and flat-screen TVs. Bathrooms are standard but feature premium Crabtree & Evelyn bath products.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The on-site restaurant, Antica Ristorante, serves Italian cuisine and a hearty breakfast buffet. The hotel features a comprehensive, 24-hour fitness center equipped with modern cardio and weight-training equipment. Guests are famously welcomed at check-in with the brand's signature warm chocolate chip cookie.

**User Review Highlights:** The welcoming staff and the beloved warm cookie at check-in are frequent highlights. Reviewers also heavily praise the location's proximity to the Staten Island Ferry, Battery Park, and the vibrant outdoor dining scene on nearby Stone Street.

## 7. Radisson Hotel Wall Street

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Tucked into a narrow footprint on William Street, this 18-story property is an efficient, business-first hotel. The architecture is modern and unassuming, blending seamlessly into the towering financial buildings surrounding it. The lobby is compact, designed for quick transactions and minimal lingering.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** The 289 rooms have a clean, contemporary design with a neutral color palette. Standard amenities include free high-speed Wi-Fi, flat-screen TVs, and work desks. A unique feature for a hotel in this class is that select rooms and suites offer kitchenettes with mini-fridges and microwaves, making it suitable for slightly longer stays.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The hotel is home to The Bailey Pub & Brasserie, a popular spot that blends traditional Irish pub warmth with a modern American menu, serving as a reliable watering hole for both guests and local finance workers. A standard fitness center and versatile meeting spaces are also located on-site.

**User Review Highlights:** Corporate travelers are the primary reviewers here, and they consistently praise the seamless check-in process and the absolute convenience to Wall Street offices. The comfort of the beds, the cleanliness of the facilities, and the lively atmosphere at The Bailey Pub are also common positive points.

## 8. Millennium Downtown New York

**Property Overview & Architecture:** This is a massive, established 55-story glass-tower hotel directly overlooking the World Trade Center campus. Because of its sheer scale (over 560 rooms), it is a major hub for large tour groups, airline crews, and corporate blocks. The architecture maximizes the sweeping views of the Oculus, the 9/11 Memorial pools, and the Hudson River.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** Rooms are classically styled and slightly more traditional than newer boutique properties. The primary amenity of these rooms is the view; corner rooms feature wall-to-wall windows offering some of the most striking unobstructed sightlines in Lower Manhattan. Rooms include standard work desks, flat-screen TVs, coffee makers, and comfortable, reliable bedding.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The hotel features Liquid Assets, a lobby bar offering craft cocktails and light bites, and Church & Dey, a restaurant serving American cuisine with a focus on seasonal ingredients. The property also houses extensive meeting and event spaces, catering to large-scale conferences.

**User Review Highlights:** The views are the undisputed star of the reviews; guests rave about waking up to aerial sights of the Oculus and One World Trade. Reviewers also appreciate the hotel's ability to efficiently handle large amounts of luggage, the proximity to Century 21 for shopping, and the overall safety of the area.

## 9. The Beekman, A Thompson Hotel

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Housed in the historic 1883 Temple Court building, The Beekman is an architectural masterpiece and a protected New York City landmark. The defining feature is its breathtaking nine-story Victorian atrium, framed by wrought-iron balconies and capped with a pyramidal glass skylight. The interior design by Martin Brudnizki leans heavily into old-world glamour, featuring vintage Persian rugs, fringed lamps, and curated curiosities that give it an upscale, cinematic feel.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** The 287 rooms blend vintage elegance with modern luxury. They feature custom-designed oak beds with leather headboards, aged oak floors, and bespoke furnishings. Amenities include cocktail tables, craft cocktail carts, and curated artwork. The marble bathrooms are opulent, featuring oversized walk-in rain showers and premium D.S. & Durga bath amenities.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The Beekman is a major culinary destination. It houses Temple Court, an acclaimed restaurant by celebrity chef Tom Colicchio, serving classic American dishes in a stunning, stained-glass setting. The Bar Room, located at the base of the atrium, is one of the most visually stunning lounges in the city. The hotel also features a bi-level fitness center.

**User Review Highlights:** Guests frequently describe the hotel as "breathtaking" and "magical," highlighting the historic atrium and the luxurious, romantic ambiance. The world-class dining, impeccable five-star service, and the meticulous attention to architectural detail are consistently praised in reviews.

## 10. The Wall Street Hotel

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Located in the historic Tontine Building—the original site of the New York Stock Exchange—this property was developed by the Paspaley family (renowned Australian pearling pioneers). The architecture and design reflect a refined, residential luxury, blending historic Beaux-Arts architecture with bright, airy, and sophisticated modern interiors. It has a decidedly polished, upper-crust boutique feel.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** The rooms are exceptionally elegant, featuring a soft color palette of seafoam greens, creams, and warm golds. They are furnished with custom pieces, plush seating, and heavily curated minibars. The tech is state-of-the-art, with intuitive room controls and large casting-ready TVs. The bathrooms are a highlight, featuring heated floors, luxurious soaking tubs, and Acqua di Parma bath products.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The culinary centerpiece is La Marchande, a modernized French brasserie helmed by Michelin-starred chef John Fraser. The Lounge on Pearl serves as an elegant all-day living room for high tea and evening cocktails. The hotel also offers a curated rooftop terrace and a 24-hour fitness center equipped with the latest wellness technology.

**User Review Highlights:** Reviewers laud this property for delivering a true five-star luxury experience without feeling overly stiff. Guests highlight the massive, beautifully appointed bathrooms, the exquisite dining at La Marchande, and the highly proactive, polished concierge and front desk staff.

## 11. Four Points by Sheraton Downtown

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Located on Platt Street, this is a streamlined, purpose-built modern hotel that prioritizes functional comfort. The building is a standard contemporary high-rise, designed with the practical needs of the "grab-and-go" business traveler in mind. The public spaces are bright, clean, and uncomplicated.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** Rooms are designed for efficiency, featuring the brand's signature Four Points by Sheraton Four Comfort Bed. They include dedicated workspaces with ergonomic chairs, fast and free Wi-Fi, laptop-sized safes, and flat-screen TVs. While the footprint is standard for NYC, the layout is smart, and the bathrooms are modern and brightly lit with walk-in showers.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The hotel features the High Key Bar, a casual spot for guests to grab a craft beer (highlighting the brand's Best Brews program) or a quick bite. There is a standard fitness center on-site, and the lobby offers complimentary coffee in the mornings.

**User Review Highlights:** Guests appreciate the quietness of the street despite the central location. Positive reviews frequently highlight the fast and reliable internet, the comfortable signature bedding, and the consistently clean, modern condition of the rooms and bathrooms.

## 12. Club Quarters World Trade Center

**Property Overview & Architecture:** Located on Washington Street, this hotel operates on a slightly different model, catering heavily to corporate members while still welcoming the public. The design is strictly business-oriented, focusing on productivity and convenience over flashy aesthetics. It features a "Club Living Room" concept in the lobby—a quiet, professional space designed for working or casual meetings.

**Room & Suite Amenities:** The rooms are highly functional and contemporary. They prioritize robust workstations with good lighting and ergonomic chairs, lightning-fast Wi-Fi, and multiple charging ports. Standard rooms are compact but efficient, featuring complimentary Nespresso machines. The larger Superior rooms and suites include small kitchenettes with microwaves and mini-fridges, making them ideal for corporate project work or longer stays.

**Dining & Leisure Facilities:** The standout feature is the "View of the World" terrace and restaurant located on the 20th floor, offering spectacular vantage points of the 9/11 Memorial and One World Trade Center. The hotel also provides complimentary purified water stations on every floor, a fitness center, and the Club Living Room with free snacks and coffee.

**User Review Highlights:** Practicality is the main theme in user reviews. Guests highly value the free laundry facilities, the in-room kitchenettes, and the excellent communal workspaces. The sweeping views from the top-floor terrace and the absolute proximity to the WTC transit hub are also major positive highlights.
